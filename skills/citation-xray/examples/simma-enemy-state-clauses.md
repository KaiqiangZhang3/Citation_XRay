# Example: Enemy State Clauses in UN Charter Commentaries

Target argument: enemy State clauses are obsolete, transitional, no longer practically significant, no longer applicable to former enemy States after UN admission, or are subject to deletion/reform efforts.

## Countable pathways

- direct obsolescence / lost practical significance
- transitional character
- former enemy States’ admission to the UN
- loss of enemy State status
- efforts to delete/repeal/remove enemy State clauses
- UN reform context concerning enemy State clauses

## Non-countable pathways

- pure historical background
- contrary/reserved views
- Soviet or other positions asserting the clauses still retain significance
- general use-of-force or jus cogens background unless directly tied to enemy State clauses
- internal cross-references already counted elsewhere
